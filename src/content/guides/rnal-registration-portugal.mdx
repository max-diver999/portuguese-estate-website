## What is RNAL and why registration matters for investors

RNAL stands for Registo Nacional de Alojamento Local. It is the public register maintained under Portuguese tourism law where every legal Alojamento Local operation must appear with a unique registration number. The number links three things: the physical unit (fraction and artigo matricial), the operator (natural or legal person identified by NIF), and the declared AL category (apartamento, moradia, quartos, or other sub-types).

Registration is not optional decoration for platform listings. It is the legal precondition for offering furnished tourist accommodation for short stays. Long-term residential leases under the NRAU do not require RNAL. Hotels and apart-hotels follow different establishment licensing. AL sits in the middle: residential stock used for tourist nights, regulated nationally and capped locally.

For property buyers, RNAL status is embedded in price. A €395,000 Lisbon one-bedroom marketed with "€2,100/month Airbnb equivalent" carries a regulatory premium. If RMAL containment blocks new registration and sale-triggered expiry applies, that premium evaporates at escritura. The asset reprices to long-term rent of perhaps €1,550/month. Registration risk belongs in the same diligence pass as penhoras, licença de utilização, and condominium minutes, covered in our [due diligence Portugal property checklist](/guides/due-diligence-portugal-property/).

Guests, neighbours, tax authorities, and competing operators can search registrations by address at alojamentolocal.pt. If the fraction you intend to buy does not show an active entry matching the seller's claims, treat informal operation as enforcement exposure, not transferable goodwill.

| Register layer | Scope | Investor relevance |

| RNAL (national) | All Portugal AL operations | Mandatory portal registration and number display |
| RMAL (Lisbon municipal) | Lisbon additional layer | Density caps and sale-triggered expiry |
| Finanças Category B | Tax on AL income | Independent of but cross-checked with RNAL |
| Condominium assembly | Building-level tourist ban | Can block registration regardless of parish cap |

## Decree-Law 76/2024: the framework behind RNAL portal rules

Decree-Law 76/2024, published in the Diário da República on 14 October 2024, reformed Alojamento Local after the November 2023 national registration freeze. For RNAL portal users, four changes matter most.

First, the freeze lifted. New applications could again be filed where municipal policy permits. Investors who paused conversions in 2024 could resume, but only in parishes and buildings where density caps and condominium rules allow.

Second, automatic five-year expiry was removed. Under earlier rules, registrations could lapse on a calendar cycle. Decree-Law 76/2024 shifted to a permanent registration model: valid entries persist until revoked, non-compliant, or extinguished by specific events such as sale in Lisbon containment. Buyers who were told "the licence expires in 2027 anyway" need updated advice. Calendar age alone no longer kills registration.

Third, liability insurance became a hard compliance gate on the RNAL portal from March 2025. Upload is not a one-time checkbox. Lapsed insurance can suspend registration even when municipal density would otherwise permit operation.

Fourth, municipalities received stronger tools to define containment zones, density thresholds, and transfer rules. National law sets the portal workflow; local execution determines whether your filing succeeds.

| DL 76/2024 change | Effective signal | RNAL portal impact |
|---|---|---|
| Freeze lifted | Oct 2024 | New submissions accepted where municipal policy allows |
| 5-year expiry removed | Oct 2024 | Registrations persist until event-driven extinction |
| Insurance mandatory | Mar 2025 | Upload required; lapse risks suspension |
| Municipal containment powers | 2025–2026 | Lisbon RMAL and other câmaras block new filings |

Citability rule for buyers: an STR thesis is only as strong as the RNAL number tied to the exact fraction in the caderneta predial, verified on the portal on the CPCV date, not at initial viewing weeks earlier.

## Prerequisites before you open the RNAL portal

RNAL registration is an administrative process, but refusals usually trace to missing property-law foundations, not portal bugs. Complete these before filing.

### Operator identification

You need a Portuguese NIF (número de identificação fiscal). Foreign owners obtain NIF through Finanças or a fiscal representative before purchase completion. Corporate operators use the company NIF. The NIF on the registration must match who receives AL income for tax purposes. Mismatches trigger Finanças cross-checks. See our [NIF Portugal property purchase guide](/guides/nif-portugal-property-purchase/) for non-resident setup.

### Property identifiers

The portal requires identifiers matching Finanças and land registry records:

- Artigo matricial (fiscal property article)
- Fraction number in horizontal property buildings
- Full address aligned with caderneta predial
- AL type selection (apartamento, moradia, quartos, etc.)

A common rejection cause is filing for the building entrance address while renting a specific apartment fraction. Registration attaches to the fraction, not the marketing photo of the lobby.

### Licença de utilização and habitability

AL registration assumes legal habitation-compatible use. The licença de utilização confirms the unit was approved for residential occupation. Illegal construction, missing habitation licence, or commercial-only classification blocks or delays RNAL approval. Do not conflate AL registration with curing unauthorized works.

### Condominium consent

Buildings under horizontal property law may require assembly authorization for tourist use. Decree-Law 76/2024 strengthened condominium powers: buildings with four or more residential fractions can pass resolutions blocking new AL registrations with a two-thirds supermajority. Some existing registrations continue, but new filings fail if a valid notarially registered ban exists. Request condominium minutes and registered bylaws before portal submission.

### Insurance policy ready for upload

Since March 2025, civil-liability insurance covering guest-related risks must be uploaded with the application and renewed on the portal annually. Standard multi-risk homeowner policies often exclude paying guest activity. Purchase AL-specific or commercial liability coverage before filing.

| Prerequisite | Typical source | Portal failure if missing |
|---|---|---|
| NIF | Finanças / fiscal rep | Cannot authenticate or complete operator profile |
| Artigo matricial + fraction | Caderneta predial | Registration linked to wrong unit |
| Licença de utilização | Câmara / seller | Filing rejected or inspection ordered |
| Condominium consent | Assembly minutes | Municipal or national refusal |
| Liability insurance | Insurer certificate | Non-compliant from Mar 2025 onward |

## RNAL portal registration: step-by-step workflow

The national portal at alojamentolocal.pt is the single entry point for RNAL registration portugal filings. Municipalities may require parallel steps, but the RNAL number issues through this national layer.

### Step 1: Authenticate with NIF

Create or access your operator account using Portuguese digital authentication (Chave Móvel, Cartão de Cidadão) or approved credentials linked to your NIF. Corporate operators file under the company NIF with authorised signatory access. If you are a foreign buyer still establishing fiscal presence, complete NIF setup first. Filing under a third-party manager's NIF without clear contractual transfer at purchase creates transfer risk later.

### Step 2: Select AL category and capacity

Choose the unit type that matches physical reality:

- **Apartamento**: self-contained apartment in horizontal property
- **Moradia**: whole house or villa
- **Quartos**: rooms in owner-occupied dwelling
- **Other sub-categories** as defined in tourism regulations

Capacity (maximum guests) must reflect habitability and safety rules. Overstating capacity invites inspection failure and fine exposure.

### Step 3: Enter property data matching official records

Input artigo matricial, fraction, parish, and address exactly as they appear on the caderneta predial and energy certificate where required. Cross-check against the seller's documentation before escritura if you are buying an operating unit. Typos between portal and registry delay approval by weeks.

### Step 4: Upload mandatory insurance certificate

Attach the civil-liability policy meeting Decree-Law 76/2024 requirements. The certificate should show coverage period, insured party NIF, and insured address matching the registration. Set a calendar reminder 30 days before expiry. Lapsed upload suspends registration.

### Step 5: Attach condominium and municipal documents

Where required, upload assembly minutes approving tourist use, municipal pre-approval letters, or inspection reports. Lisbon may require RMAL-layer steps in parallel with national RNAL. Algarve câmaras such as Lagos or Albufeira may invoice local fees triggered at filing.

### Step 6: Pay municipal charges if invoiced

Some municipalities charge registration or inspection fees ranging from zero to €350 or more. Payment status may gate final approval. Keep receipts for accountant and resale due diligence files.

### Step 7: Receive RNAL number and publish compliance

Upon approval, the portal issues the RNAL number. Display it on all booking listings and inside the unit as required. Update Finanças activity classification for Category B AL income. There is no interim legal status allowing paid guests while "awaiting number."

```
RNAL filing sequence (simplified)

NIF ready → Portal login → AL type selected
    → Property IDs entered → Insurance uploaded
        → Condominium/municipal docs attached
            → Fees paid (if any) → RNAL number issued
                → Listings live + Category B tax alignment
```

Processing time ranges from same-day in permissive Algarve municipalities to 8–12 weeks in Lisbon or Porto historic zones with inspection queues. Plan furnishing and platform launch only after the number is active.

## Mandatory insurance on the RNAL portal since March 2025

Decree-Law 76/2024 made liability insurance non-optional for all active RNAL registrations from March 2025. This is a operational shift many owners missed: registration obtained in 2022 or 2023 without current insurance upload is vulnerable to suspension in 2026 even if guest reviews are excellent.

The policy must cover civil liability arising from guest stays: injury, property damage, and related third-party claims. Minimum coverage thresholds should be confirmed with an insurer familiar with AL activity. Budget €150–€400 per year for a typical one- or two-bedroom urban apartment, more for villas with pools or multiple units.

Annual renewal requires portal re-upload. Treat insurance like IMI: a fixed carry cost in net yield models. Buyers completing under [CPCV promissory contract](/guides/cpcv-promissory-contract-portugal/) terms should require seller proof of current RNAL insurance status before deposit, mirroring licença de utilização checks.

| Insurance topic | Practical rule |
|---|---|
| Policy type | AL/commercial liability, not standard home-only |
| Upload timing | At initial filing and each renewal |
| Non-compliance risk | Suspension or revocation of RNAL entry |
| Buyer diligence | Verify upload date on portal before CPCV |
| Net yield impact | €150–€400/year off net cash |

Standard homeowner policies that exclude "commercial activity" do not satisfy the gate. Inform your broker explicitly that the unit will hold paying tourist guests registered on RNAL.

## Municipal caps: when the portal accepts filing but the câmara does not

National portal access does not guarantee approval. Municipal density rules under RMAL and related frameworks can block new RNAL registrations even when building and condominium consent are clean.

### Lisbon RMAL containment

Lisbon implements the strictest caps. When licensed AL units represent 10% or more of total housing stock in a freguesia, no new registrations are issued. This is absolute containment. Between 5% and 10%, new licences are exceptional only with municipal justification.

Sale-triggered expiry adds buyer risk: in containment zones, RNAL registrations for apartments and houses expire upon sale. The buyer must apply fresh and will be refused if the parish remains at or above the 10% threshold. Deep mapping and parish tables live in [Lisbon Alojamento Local containment zones](/guides/lisbon-alojamento-local-containment-zones/).

### Porto, Algarve, and other câmaras

Porto restricts the historic core while outer parishes remain more open subject to condominium consent. The Algarve is broadly permissive compared with Lisbon, but each câmara maintains local fees, inspections, and occasional historic-centre limits. Platform density in Lagos or Albufeira does not prove your fraction can register. Verify on the portal and with the câmara for the specific address.

| Porto historic core | Restricted, inspection-heavy | Transfer verification critical |
| Porto outer parishes | More open | Condominium bylaws still binding |

## Permanent registration model: no automatic five-year expiry

Before Decree-Law 76/2024, investors planned around five-year licence renewal cycles. That calendar model ended. Valid RNAL registrations now follow permanent registration logic until an extinguishing event occurs.

Extinguishing events include:

- Operator request to cancel
- Revocation for non-compliance or missing insurance upload
- Condominium assembly ban preventing renewal after lapse
- Sale-triggered expiry in Lisbon RMAL containment for apartments and houses
- Municipal revocation in exceptional cases

This permanence increases the scarcity value of grandfathered registrations in capped parishes, but it also means buyers pay STR premiums for units where transfer dies at sale. Always distinguish "active registration today" from "registration transferable to me after escritura."

Sellers marketing "licence valid until 2029" under old law misunderstand current rules. Ask for portal screenshots dated within 14 days of CPCV and lawyer confirmation of transfer mechanics.

## RNAL transfer on sale: what the portal shows versus what you inherit

Transfer is the highest-friction RNAL topic in 2026 transactions. The portal displays who holds registration today. It does not guarantee that you inherit that status tomorrow.

### When continuity may work

Outside Lisbon sale-triggered containment, a buyer may maintain AL activity if:

- The câmara confirms the new owner can hold registration without a cap-blocked new application
- Condominium bylaws permit tourist use without a fresh assembly vote on change of owner
- Insurance is reissued in the buyer's NIF or company name and uploaded
- Seller cooperates on operator update or coordinated cancellation within 48 hours of escritura

Mandate seller cooperation in the [CPCV promissory contract](/guides/cpcv-promissory-contract-portugal/). Legal fees for coordination are cheaper than three months of illegal operation.

### When registration dies at sale

In Lisbon RMAL containment zones, apartments and houses lose AL status on sale. The buyer inherits no RNAL number. If the parish is at 10%+ licensed density, fresh application fails. The asset reprices to long-term rental value immediately.

Even outside Lisbon, quartos registrations tied to personal operator residence may not transfer with freehold. Corporate structures holding registration may require share deals instead of asset deals, triggering separate corporate due diligence.

### Price adjustment logic

If marketing priced STR income but transfer fails, renegotiate against long-term gross yield before deposit. Example: €440,000 Lisbon apartment marketed at 6.2% STR gross (€27,280/year). Long-term market rent €1,650/month (€19,800/year, 4.5% gross). The €7,480 annual gap capitalised at 5% implies roughly €149,600 theoretical adjustment before costs. Real negotiations rarely move that far, but deposit without RNAL verification invites asymmetric loss.

## Post-registration compliance: keeping the portal entry active

Approval is the start, not the finish. Operators must maintain:

- **Insurance renewal** uploaded before expiry
- **RNAL updates within 30 days** of material changes (ownership, operator entity, capacity, address corrections)
- **Fire safety and habitability** standards for inspections
- **Category B tax alignment** with actual guest income
- **Platform number display** on every listing

Failure triggers fines from €2,500 to €25,000 or more under tourism and tax law, plus condominium litigation. Finanças cross-matches platform payouts with RNAL status and Category B filings.

Annual compliance calendar:

| Month | Action |
|---|---|
| January | Review prior-year Category B filings with accountant |
| 30 days before insurance expiry | Renew policy and upload to portal |
| Quarterly | Verify condominium minutes for new tourist-ban votes |
| Pre-high-season | Test booking compliance (number on all listings) |
| On ownership change | Coordinate portal update at escritura |

## Common RNAL portal rejection reasons and fixes

Understanding refusals saves months of vacancy.

**Fraction mismatch**: Filed for wrong apartment number. Fix: Re-file with caderneta predial exact fraction.

**Missing licença de utilização**: Cannot prove habitation use. Fix: Obtain municipal habitation documentation or cure illegal status before AL filing.

**Condominium ban**: Registered assembly resolution blocks tourist activity. Fix: Pivot to long-term letting; AL not viable in that building.

**Parish containment**: Lisbon or other RMAL cap at 10%+. Fix: No new registration path; reprice asset.

**Insurance deficient**: Policy excludes guest liability or wrong insured address. Fix: Replace policy and re-upload.

**Operator NIF mismatch**: Company versus personal inconsistency. Fix: Align ownership, tax, and portal operator.

**Pending municipal inspection**: Unit queued for visit. Fix: Complete inspection, then resubmit.

If rejected, the portal usually states the reason. Engage a local lawyer or compliance agent (€400–€1,200 for first filing support) when the reason implicates condominium law or containment interpretation.

## Buyer due diligence: verify RNAL before you price STR income

Every STR-priced purchase should run this sequence before CPCV:

1. Pull RNAL entry at alojamentolocal.pt for the exact fraction, not the building
2. Confirm operator NIF matches seller or explain transfer plan
3. Check insurance upload currency on the portal
4. Map Lisbon parish against RMAL containment publications
5. Read condominium minutes for tourist bans or supermajority requirements
6. Obtain lawyer written opinion on sale-triggered expiry
7. Model net yield with legal AL and with long-term fallback

If step 1 shows no active entry, do not rely on seller promises of "pending renewal." If step 4 shows absolute containment, assume new registration refusal unless lawyer obtains exceptional written pre-approval.

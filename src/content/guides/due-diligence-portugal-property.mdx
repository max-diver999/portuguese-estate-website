Due diligence on Portugal property is the phase that separates a secure freehold purchase from an expensive mistake. The Portuguese system relies on public registries, but those registries do not automatically surface every problem: illegal extensions, unpaid condominium charges, blocked Alojamento Local licences, and court attachments only become visible when your lawyer requests the right documents in the right sequence.

This guide is written for buyers at the bottom of the funnel: you have identified a property, you are preparing to sign the CPCV, and you need a structured checklist your lawyer can execute. It complements the wider purchase path in our [how to buy property in Portugal step by step](/guides/how-to-buy-property-portugal-step-by-step/) guide, the deposit-stage contract review in our [CPCV promissory contract guide](/guides/cpcv-promissory-contract-portugal/), and financing checks in our [non-resident mortgage Portugal guide](/guides/non-resident-mortgage-portugal/).

## What does property due diligence in Portugal actually prove?

Due diligence confirms three things before money becomes hard to recover: the seller owns what they claim to sell, the property is legally usable for your intended purpose, and no hidden liabilities transfer to you at the escritura. Portugal has no centralised pre-contract disclosure pack like some US states. The buyer, through a licensed lawyer, must assemble evidence from Finanças, the Conservatória do Registo Predial, the câmara municipal, and, where relevant, the condominium administrator.

| Due diligence outcome | What it protects | Primary source document |
|---|---|---|
| Clean title | You receive unencumbered ownership | Certidão de teor |
| Legal use classification | Residential vs commercial vs rural use is valid | Licença de utilização |
| Fiscal baseline for taxes | IMI, IMT simulation, AIMI exposure | Caderneta predial |
| No court attachments | Tax debts and litigation do not follow the deed | Penhoras search |
| No hidden construction | Registered area matches reality | Licença + approved plans + survey |
| No inherited communal debt | Apartment purchase does not absorb neighbour defaults | Condominium accounts |
| Rental strategy viable | AL income assumptions hold | RNAL register + municipal policy |

If any row in this table fails, the correct response is to renegotiate price, require the seller to cure the defect before CPCV, or walk away. Deposits are forfeited when buyers sign before checks complete.

## The core document stack: caderneta predial, certidão de teor, licença de utilização

Your lawyer requests these three documents in every mainstream urban or suburban purchase. Together they form the minimum evidence set without which no informed buyer should pay a deposit.

### Caderneta predial urbana

The caderneta predial is issued by Autoridade Tributária (Finanças). It is a fiscal extract, not a title deed, but it anchors tax calculations and often reveals the first red flags.

What the caderneta shows:

- **VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário):** the fiscal value used for IMI annual property tax and IMT simulations
- **Artigo matricial:** the property's tax registration number
- **Área bruta privativa and dependent areas:** registered internal and dependent floor space
- **Tipo de prédio:** urban, rustic, or mixed classification
- **Current registered owner:** must match the seller on the CPCV

Cross-check the caderneta against the listing floor plan and, for older stock, a measured survey. A 20-square-metre discrepancy between registered area and physical layout is one of the most common signals of construção ilegal. The caderneta also feeds your IMT exposure model: under DL 97/2026, non-residents completing on or after 1 September 2026 pay a flat 7.5% IMT on the declared transaction value or tax assessment, whichever is higher. See our dedicated [IMT tax for non-residents 2026](/guides/imt-tax-non-resident-portugal-2026/) guide for worked examples.

### Certidão de teor

The certidão de teor is the authoritative title certificate from the Conservatória do Registo Predial. Where the caderneta is fiscal, the certidão is legal.

Your lawyer verifies:

- Registered owner matches the seller's identity documents
- No active mortgages (hipoteca) unless you are assuming one by agreement
- No usufruct (usufruto) or life-interest rights blocking full ownership
- No pending registrations that could priority-rank ahead of your deed
- Description of the property matches the unit you are viewing (fraction letter, floor, parking space)

Request a certidão updated within 30 days of CPCV signing. Older certificates miss registrations filed after issuance.

### Licença de utilização

Licença de utilização (or licença de habitabilidade on older stock) is the municipal confirmation that the building legally matches its registered use. A residential apartment marketed for holiday letting must carry a valid residential use licence. A commercial unit converted without municipal approval creates financing and insurance problems.

| Licence status | Buyer risk level | Typical remedy |
|---|---|---|
| Valid licence, matches current use | Low | Proceed |
| Licence exists but area on licence smaller than built area | High | Seller regularises or price reduction |
| Licence missing on pre-1980 stock | Medium to high | Lawyer assesses amnesty pathways; lender may refuse |
| Licence shows commercial, marketed as residential | High | Reclassification required before mortgage |
| New-build without final licence | Medium | Hold deposit until licença definitiva issued |

Without a valid licence, Câmara Municipal can impose fines, block utility connections, and complicate resale. Never accept verbal assurances from agents that a property is "grandfathered" without written municipal confirmation.

## Penhoras and encumbrances: what attaches to the title

Penhoras are court-ordered attachments on property, typically triggered by unpaid taxes, social security debts, supplier litigation, or divorce proceedings. They do not always appear in marketing materials. They do appear on a properly conducted due diligence search.

### Types of charges to screen

| Charge type | Registry location | Cleared by |
|---|---|---|
| Mortgage (hipoteca) | Conservatória | Seller pays off at escritura |
| Tax attachment (penhora fiscal) | Conservatória + Finanças | Seller settles debt; AT releases |
| Court attachment (penhora judicial) | Conservatória | Court order + creditor agreement |
| Condominium debt (dívida condominial) | Not always on title; administrator records | Seller pays arrears before deed |
| Sequestration (seqüestro) | Conservatória | Legal resolution required |

Your lawyer runs a penhoras search through the enforcement agent portal and cross-references Finanças for outstanding IMI or IRS liabilities tied to the property. The CPCV should state that the seller delivers a property free of encumbrances, or specifies exactly which charges will be discharged from the sale proceeds at the notary.

A property with active penhoras is not automatically unpurchasable, but the seller must prove a clearance path before you transfer a deposit. If clearance requires months of litigation, treat the deal as off-market until resolved.

## Construção ilegal: the most expensive surprise in Portuguese resale stock

Construção ilegal covers any built structure or extension without approved municipal licensing: enclosed balconies, rooftop terraces, basement conversions, pool houses, and extra bedrooms carved from storage space. Portugal's coastal and village markets contain decades of incremental building that never entered the registry.

Due diligence on construction legality follows a three-way comparison:

1. **Licença de utilização** approved areas and use class
2. **Approved architectural plans** on file at the câmara (plantas aprovadas)
3. **Physical inspection** or measured survey comparing reality to both

| Signal | Likely issue | Due diligence action |
|---|---|---|
| Bedroom count exceeds licence | Unlicensed partition | Request regularisation quote from architect |
| Pool exists but not on plans | Unlicensed structure | Check PDM coastal or rustic land rules |
| Enclosed terrace adds habitable area | Common in Algarve apartments | Compare área bruta on caderneta vs plans |
| Recent cosmetic renovation | May hide structural or illegal works | Structural survey €800–1,500 |
| Rural ruin with new wing | High regularisation risk | Full PDM and IMPIC file review |

Regularisation (legalização) is sometimes possible through simplified municipal procedures, but there is no guarantee. Fines under the RJUE urbanisation framework can exceed €30,000 on severe cases, and demolition orders are issued where safety or coastal protection rules apply. Price renegotiation should reflect the full cost of regularisation plus legal risk premium, not just the seller's optimistic estimate.

For investment buyers comparing regions, note that illegal extensions cluster differently: Algarve holiday apartments and Lisbon attic conversions are frequent; Porto historic-centre retrofits carry heritage constraints that make regularisation slower. Regional market context sits in our [Algarve property investment guide](/guides/algarve-property-investment-guide/) and [Lisbon property investment guide](/guides/lisbon-property-investment-guide/).

## Condominium debts and internal rules

Apartment and townhouse purchases require condominium due diligence beyond the title registry. Under Portuguese horizontal property law (propriedade horizontal), certain debts follow the unit even when not registered on the certidão de teor at the moment of search.

Your lawyer requests from the administrator (administrador do condomínio):

- Certificate of no debt (certidão de não dívida) or equivalent written confirmation
- Last 12 months of meeting minutes (atas)
- Current monthly quota (quota condominial) and any approved extraordinary assessments
- Building insurance status and any pending litigation against the condominium
- Rules on short-term letting, Airbnb, and pets if relevant to your strategy

| Condominium finding | Impact on buyer | Negotiation lever |
|---|---|---|
| Seller owes 6+ months quotas | You may inherit enforcement action | Deduct arrears from completion payment |
| Approved façade works not yet paid | Special assessment incoming | Reduce price by your share of levy |
| Minutes show AL ban voted | Holiday-let strategy blocked | Re-price or exit |
| Structural defects noted in minutes | Reserve fund may be inadequate | Survey + price adjustment |
| Pending lawsuit vs developer | Uncertain liability | Delay CPCV until resolved |

Never rely on the seller's verbal claim that condominium accounts are current. Administrators issue written certificates within days when asked by a lawyer.

## AL and RNAL check: when rental income is part of the thesis

If you buy to operate Alojamento Local (AL) short-term rentals, due diligence extends into tourism licensing. The RNAL (Registo Nacional de Alojamento Local) is the national register at alojamentolocal.pt. Each active licence ties to a specific property fraction and municipal approval.

Verify four layers:

1. **RNAL status:** licence number active, not suspended or cancelled
2. **Municipal policy:** câmara rules on new licences, transfers, and containment zones (zonas de contenção)
3. **Condominium permission:** building bylaws or recent owner votes restricting AL
4. **IMI and tax classification:** AL activity may affect expense deductions; non-residents pay 28% on rental income

Since 2023, Lisbon has designated multiple parishes where new AL licences and transfers are restricted or blocked. The Algarve remains broadly more permissive, but Lagos, Albufeira, and Faro each maintain local caps. A property marketed with "existing Airbnb income" without a transferable RNAL entry is a valuation trap.

| AL due diligence result | Investment implication |
|---|---|
| Active RNAL, transferable, condominium silent | Income thesis intact; confirm in CPCV |
| Active RNAL but municipality blocks transfer | Re-underwrite without AL premium |
| Expired RNAL | Re-application required; timing uncertain |
| No RNAL but neighbours operate AL | Do not assume precedent applies to you |
| Condominium voted AL ban | Long-term let only; adjust yield model |

Cross-link your rental yield assumptions with our [Alojamento Local licence Portugal guide](/guides/alojamento-local-license-portugal/) and [Portugal rental yield guide](/guides/portugal-rental-yield-guide/) only after AL status is confirmed in writing.

## IMT exposure: the tax due diligence layer

Due diligence is not only legal; it is financial. IMT (Imposto Municipal sobre Transmissões) is calculated at completion based on the higher of the declared purchase price or the tax authority's reference value. Your lawyer runs the official AT/CIMT simulator during due diligence, not after CPCV.

Key IMT due diligence questions:

| Question | Why it matters | Document source |
|---|---|---|
| Will you be tax resident at completion? | Residents keep progressive bands; non-residents pay 7.5% flat from Sep 2026 | NIF status + 183-day forecast |
| Does VPT on caderneta sit far below price? | AT may assess IMT on higher reference value | Caderneta predial |
| Is property classified primary vs secondary housing? | Affects simulation inputs pre-reform | Seller declaration + lawyer review |
| Are you buying through a company? | Corporate structures face distinct rules | Company NIF + tax counsel |
| Can you qualify for IMT refund within 24 months? | Changes effective cost if you relocate | Tax residency plan documented |

On a €500,000 Lisbon apartment, a non-resident completing after 1 September 2026 pays €37,500 IMT before stamp duty. Completing in August 2026 under the old progressive scale might save €15,000 to €20,000 depending on bracket boundaries, but only if every legal check is clean. Never accelerate escritura to beat the IMT deadline while skipping penhoras or licence verification; one hidden charge erases the tax saving.

Full mechanics, refund windows, and bracket tables live in our [IMT tax non-resident Portugal 2026](/guides/imt-tax-non-resident-portugal-2026/) guide. Total cash-to-close modelling belongs in the [cost of buying property in Portugal](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-portugal/) breakdown.

## Lawyer scope, independence, and cost (1% to 1.5%)

Portuguese law does not require you to appoint a lawyer, but international buyers treating due diligence as optional consistently overpay for defective stock. Your lawyer must be independent: not the seller's counsel, not the agent's in-house referral with a commission split unless you accept that conflict explicitly.

Standard due diligence and conveyancing scope:

- Order and interpret caderneta predial, certidão de teor, licença de utilização, certificado energético
- Penhoras and encumbrance search
- Condominium certificate and minutes review
- RNAL and municipal AL policy check when requested
- IMT and stamp duty simulation; payment coordination
- CPCV drafting with protective clauses (mortgage suspensive, penalty for delay, inventory annex)
- Escritura attendance or power of attorney execution
- Post-completion registration confirmation

**Fee benchmark:** 1% to 1.5% of purchase price on straightforward urban apartments and villas. Examples:

| Purchase price | Legal fee at 1% | Legal fee at 1.5% | Typical complexity |
|---:|---:|---:|---|
| €250,000 | €2,500 | €3,750 | Resale apartment, clean title |
| €400,000 | €4,000 | €6,000 | Villa with AL verification |
| €650,000 | €6,500 | €9,750 | New-build with bank guarantee review |
| €1,200,000 | €12,000 | €18,000 | Rural estate + PDM zoning |

Fees above 1.5% usually reflect off-plan bank guarantee monitoring, litigation on title, corporate SPV setup, or multi-unit portfolio acquisition. Fees below 1% often mean scope excludes survey coordination, AL verification, or CPCV negotiation, read the engagement letter carefully.

The lawyer works in parallel with, not instead of, a technical survey on older stock and a fiscal representative if you are non-EU without Portuguese tax presence. ## Due diligence timeline: when each check runs

Due diligence typically spans 2 to 6 weeks between accepted offer and CPCV, depending on document retrieval speed and survey scheduling.

| Week | Action | Responsible party |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Lawyer orders caderneta, certidão, licença copies; seller grants access | Lawyer + seller |
| Week 1–2 | Penhoras search; condominium certificate request | Lawyer |
| Week 2 | RNAL and municipal AL policy check if rental strategy | Lawyer |
| Week 2–3 | Physical viewing vs plans; optional structural survey | Buyer + surveyor |
| Week 3 | IMT simulation; total cost memo to buyer | Lawyer + buyer |
| Week 3–4 | CPCV draft with suspensive conditions; deposit negotiation | Lawyer |
| Week 4+ | Deposit paid only after written clean due diligence memo | Buyer |

Off-plan purchases add developer alvará verification, IMPIC registration, and bank guarantee validation before any stage payment. Rural land adds PDM (municipal master plan) zoning and rustic registration checks.

## Buyer scenarios: decision framework before you sign CPCV

Different buyer profiles weight due diligence priorities differently. Use this framework with your lawyer's written memo, not agent verbal assurance.

| Buyer scenario | Primary due diligence focus | Proceed if | Walk away if |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lisbon apartment, long-term let | Condominium debts, AL ban votes, lease contracts in place | Clean certidão, valid licence, no penhoras | AL ban passed; seller cannot clear arrears |
| Algarve villa, holiday AL income | RNAL transfer, pool legality, coastal PDM | Active transferable RNAL; pool on approved plans | RNAL blocked; unlicensed pool with no regularisation path |
| Porto historic flat, personal use | Construção ilegal in attic, heritage restrictions | Survey matches licence area | Structural subsidence or illegal floor added |
| Silver Coast rural quinta, renovation | Rustic + urban registration split, water rights | PDM allows intended use; no seqüestro | Rustic land without buildable classification |
| New-build off-plan, 20% deposit | Bank guarantee, IMPIC, developer track record | Guarantee names you; alvará valid | Developer refuses guarantee or IMPIC missing |
| €600k+ portfolio, company SPV | IMT corporate exposure, AIMI stacking | Tax counsel signs structure memo | Title litigation pending |
| Non-resident racing pre-Sep 2026 IMT | All standard checks plus completion date certainty | Escritura credibly before 1 Sep; clean title | Any penhora or licence gap requiring months |

**Decision rule:** If the lawyer's memo lists any unresolved high-severity item (active penhora, missing licence with no regularisation path, RNAL blocked where income is priced in, material construção ilegal), default action is no CPCV. Revisit only with a price reduction that fully covers cure cost plus risk premium, or a seller-funded escrow held until clearance.

## Risks when due diligence is skipped or rushed

Agents under commission pressure sometimes frame due diligence as a delay. In Portuguese practice, it is the only phase where you can exit without losing the deposit.

| Risk | Typical financial exposure | How due diligence catches it |
|---|---|---|
| Active penhora on title | Deal blocked at notary; lost time and legal fees | Penhoras search before CPCV |
| €15,000 condominium arrears | Inherited enforcement; special levies | Administrator certificate |
| Unlicensed 30 m² extension | Regularisation €20,000+ or demolition | Plans vs survey vs caderneta |
| Blocked AL transfer | 30–50% income thesis removed | RNAL + câmara + condominium minutes |
| IMT assessed above budget | €10,000–€25,000 cash shortfall at completion | AT simulator during week 3 |
| Seller not registered owner | CPCV void; deposit recovery litigation | Certidão de teor |
| Hidden moisture or structural defect | €50,000+ remediation | Structural survey |
| Rural plot without build rights | Land worthless for intended project | PDM + rustic registration |

Portuguese law offers remedies after purchase (hidden defects claims within two years of discovery), but litigation takes 12 to 24 months and does not restore lost rental seasons or IMT savings. Prevention at due diligence stage is cheaper than court.

## Closing verification checklist

Before you authorise the CPCV deposit, confirm your lawyer has delivered written evidence on every item below.

- [ ] Caderneta predial dated within 90 days; owner matches seller
- [ ] Certidão de teor dated within 30 days; no hipoteca, penhora, or usufruto unless agreed
- [ ] Licença de utilização valid; registered area consistent with plans and inspection
- [ ] Certificado energético present and within validity period
- [ ] Penhoras search clear or cure plan funded at escritura
- [ ] Condominium certificate of no debt (if applicable)
- [ ] RNAL and municipal AL status confirmed (if rental strategy)
- [ ] IMT simulation run; total closing budget matches [cost guide](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-portugal/) assumptions
- [ ] CPCV includes mortgage suspensive clause if financing dependent
- [ ] No time pressure waiver signed unless you accept specific uncured risks in writing

Once the deposit is paid, your leverage drops sharply. The checklist above is the minimum standard for tier A purchases where title defects or regulatory blocks would materially affect value.

## How does Portugal due diligence compare with Spain for cross-border investors?

Cross-border investors weighing Iberian markets should run parallel due diligence frameworks. Portugal relies on caderneta predial, certidão de teor, and licença de utilização; Spain uses nota simple, catastro reference, and licencia de ocupación. Transfer tax timing, non-resident surcharges, and short-term rental caps differ materially. Our [Portugal vs Spain property investment comparison](/compare/portugal-vs-spain-property-investment/) maps cost, timeline, and regulation side by side.

| Due diligence element | Portugal | Spain (high-level) |
|---|---|---|
| Title certificate | Certidão de teor (Conservatória) | Nota simple (Registro de la Propiedad) |
| Fiscal extract | Caderneta predial (Finanças) | Catastro reference |
| Use licence | Licença de utilização | Licencia de ocupación / cédula |
| Court attachments | Penhoras search | Embargo on nota simple |
| Non-res transfer tax (2026) | 7.5% flat IMT from Sep 2026 (DL 97/2026) | Variable by autonomous community |
| Typical purchase timeline | 8-14 weeks | 6-12 weeks (region dependent) |

Choosing Portugal for a specific asset does not remove the need for Spain comparison on total cost and rental regulation. Run both models before CPCV.

## Portuguese Estate field notes: Q2 2026 verification standard

Portuguese Estate applies a written Q2 2026 field standard on every purchase file we review. This block summarises what our legal partners must deliver before we advise a client to pay any CPCV deposit. It extends the closing checklist above with file-level discipline we see missing in rushed 2025 market deals.

**Registry pulls (ordered fresh, never agent PDFs)**
- Certidão de teor dated within 30 days; seller matches passport and CPCV
- Caderneta predial cross-checked against licença de utilização area and approved plans
- Penhoras search with written clear result or funded cure plan at escritura
- Certificado energético valid and registered

**Apartment and condominium layer**
- Certidão de não dívida from administrador do condomínio
- Last 12 months of atas scanned for AL ban votes, façade levies, and litigation
- Quota condominial confirmed; extraordinary assessments disclosed

**Tax and completion modelling**
- IMT simulation at AT portal for completion date under DL 97/2026 (7.5% non-resident flat from 1 September 2026)
- Stamp duty at 0.8% on declared price or tax reference, whichever is higher
- Total cash-to-close memo aligned with [cost of buying property Portugal](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-portugal/)

**Investment and financing layer**
- RNAL lookup when AL income is in the price; cross-ref [Alojamento Local licence Portugal](/guides/alojamento-local-license-portugal/)
- Bank valuation gap analysis when mortgage via [non-resident mortgage Portugal](/guides/non-resident-mortgage-portugal/)
- CPCV suspensive clauses cross-ref [CPCV promissory contract Portugal](/guides/cpcv-promissory-contract-portugal/)

**Remote and cross-border layer**
- POA scope verified against [how to buy Portugal property remotely](/guides/how-to-buy-portugal-property-remotely/) if buyer absent at escritura
- Spain comparison memo optional for investors also viewing Iberian alternatives per [Portugal vs Spain property investment](/compare/portugal-vs-spain-property-investment/)

We log outcomes in a clearance memo with pass, conditional pass, or fail status. Conditional pass requires every cure item funded or price-adjusted before deposit. Fail status default is no CPCV.
